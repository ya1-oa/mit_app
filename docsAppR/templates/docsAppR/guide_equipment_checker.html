Equipment Documentation Checker

Verify that your job site photos document every billable line item — before the adjuster asks.
What does this tool do? Upload your job site photos and paste in your scope of work line items. The AI reviews every photo against a reference guide of water mitigation equipment and tells you — for each line item — whether you have adequate photographic documentation. Insurers will only pay for items documented with pictures.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Open the Equipment Checker page Log in to the app and click Equipment Checker in the left sidebar menu.
  2. Upload your job site photos Drag and drop photos onto the upload area, or click to browse and select.
    • Upload all photos from the job — the more complete, the better the analysis
    • Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, WEBP, TIFF, BMP
    • Up to 50 photos per analysis
  3. Enter your work items to verify In the Work Items to Verify text box, type or paste your scope line items — one per line. Use the format: Room Name | Description

    Example:
    # Format: Room | Line item description
    BATH DN | Vinyl tile
    BATH DN | Tear out non-salv vinyl, cut & bag
    REAR HALL DN | Tear out baseboard
    HVAC | Mask and cover register / air return
    # No room prefix is also fine:
    Emergency service call - during business hours
    You can copy line items directly from your Xactimate or Excel estimate and paste them here. The room name prefix is optional but helps organize the results table.
  4. Click "Analyze Photos" The AI will review all photos against your line items and the equipment reference guide. A progress bar tracks each stage. Analysis typically takes 30–90 seconds depending on the number of photos and line items.
  5. Review the results table Each line item gets a status badge:
BadgeMeaningWhat to do
FOUND Clear photographic evidence exists in your submitted photos No action needed — this item is well documented
PARTIAL Some evidence visible, but documentation is limited (e.g., only 1 photo, glare, distant shot) Read the note — consider adding a closer or clearer photo on your next site visit
NOT FOUND No matching photo evidence found in the submitted set This item may be at risk of denial — document it on your next visit or remove from scope
  1. Use the filter to focus on problem items Use the Status dropdown to filter the table to only show NOT FOUND or PARTIAL items. This quickly highlights what still needs to be documented.
  2. Export results as a CSV spreadsheet Click Export CSV to download a spreadsheet with all rooms, descriptions, statuses, and AI notes. Use this to share findings with your team or attach to the claim file.

Tips for Best Results

  • Upload photos before writing the scope — use the results to inform what you bill
  • Include photos of every room where work was performed, not just the damaged areas
  • Equipment photos should clearly show the device in use or in place at the job site
  • The AI uses an equipment reference guide (HUTCH EQPT LIST) automatically — no setup required
  • Paste items directly from your Excel or Xactimate estimate to save time
  • Run the check again after adding more photos to see if PARTIAL items upgrade to FOUND
Important: AI verification is a guide, not a guarantee The AI's determination is based on the photos submitted and its knowledge of standard water mitigation equipment. Always use professional judgment when reviewing results. Items marked NOT FOUND may still be payable if you have other documentation (moisture logs, invoices, etc.).

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How many line items can I check at once? There is no hard limit. The AI processes all items in a single analysis. Lists of 30–60 items work well.
  • Do I need to label my photos by room first? No — the AI reviews all photos together and matches them to your line items based on what it sees in each photo.
  • Can I re-run with more photos? Yes. Click New Check, add your additional photos alongside the originals, re-enter your line items, and run again.
  • What is the equipment reference guide? It is an internal document (HUTCH EQPT LIST) that shows photos of common water mitigation equipment. The AI uses it automatically as a reference when analyzing your photos.
  • Can I use this for every claim? Yes — run it at the end of each job before submitting the claim to catch documentation gaps early.
