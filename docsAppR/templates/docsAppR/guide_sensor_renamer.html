Sensor Image Renamer

Automatically rename and sort sensor reading photos using AI — no manual filing needed.
What does this tool do? Upload photos of your sensor displays (moisture meters and RH/Temperature/GPP loggers). The AI reads the values directly from the screen and renames each photo with those readings — then sorts them into organized folders ready for your report.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Open the Sensor Renamer page Log in to the app and click Sensor Renamer in the left sidebar menu.
  2. Upload your sensor photos Drag and drop your photos onto the upload area, or click the area to browse and select files.
    • Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, WEBP, TIFF, BMP
    • You can upload multiple photos at once (up to 50 recommended per batch)
    • Photos can be a mix of moisture meter shots and RH/T/GPP logger shots
  3. Choose your settings (optional)
    • Model — leave on the default (Sonnet) for best results; use Haiku for speed
    • Workers — controls how many photos are analyzed at the same time (default 4 is fine)
  4. Click "Process Images" A progress bar will appear. The AI reads each sensor display one by one. This typically takes 10–30 seconds for a batch of 10 photos, depending on the number of images.
  5. Review the results table When complete, a table shows every photo with the values read and which folder it was placed in.
    • Green rows = fully read and renamed
    • Orange rows = partially read (one value was unreadable)
    • Red rows = sent to NA Review for manual correction
  6. Fix any NA Review photos (if needed) If some photos could not be read automatically, they appear in the NA Review folder in the file browser. Click Correct on any NA photo, enter the values you can see on screen, and submit — the photo is moved to the correct folder automatically.
  7. Download your renamed files
    • Click Download All (ZIP) to get all folders at once
    • Or expand the file browser and download individual folders separately
    • The ZIP preserves the folder structure shown below

Tips for Best Results

  • Make sure the sensor display is clearly visible and in focus — blurry screens increase NA rate
  • Avoid photos where the display is angled sharply or glare is covering the numbers
  • Each photo should show only one sensor at a time for the most accurate readings
  • The AI rounds all readings to 1 decimal place (e.g., 65.3, 22.1)
  • If a batch has more than ~30 photos, consider splitting into two uploads for faster processing
NA Review is normal A small percentage of photos (typically those with glare, low contrast, or unusual display formats) will go to NA Review. Use the built-in correction panel — it shows a preview of the photo, pre-fills any values the AI did read, and moves the file immediately once you submit.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Can I mix MC and RH/T/GPP photos in one batch? Yes — the AI identifies the device type automatically.
  • Are my original photos kept? Yes. The tool copies your photos with new names; originals are not deleted.
  • What if I close the browser while it's processing? The task continues in the background. Return to the page and paste the task ID to resume, or simply re-upload if needed.
  • How many photos can I upload at once? Up to 50 is recommended. For larger jobs, split into batches.
